# Your 90-Day Plan to $50K/Month
### As if Brett Williams wrote it for you

Kerry. You have 25 years of building things for clients. You have Claude Code as your force multiplier. You are already doing the work. You just haven't packaged it and sold it yet.

The target: $50,000 per month. Not from Day 1. But the system you build in these 90 days is designed to get there within 6-9 months.

The math: 3 build clients at $15,000-$20,000 per project, plus 4-5 retainer clients at $5,000-$7,500/month for ongoing optimization. That is $50K-$70K/month from fewer than 10 clients.

Every build you do in Phase 1 is designed to convert into a retainer in Phase 2. That is the key difference between a project business and a revenue engine.

Here is exactly what I would do if I were you, starting today, with less than $1,000.


## The Offer

**BetterStory AI Builds + Retain**

Two tiers. One leads to the other.

**Tier 1: The Build** (project-based, $8,500 to start, rising to $15,000-$20,000)

You build a production AI system that replaces an expensive, manual process inside their business. Outbound pipelines. Document processing. Customer onboarding flows. Internal tooling. Whatever bleeds time and money. Delivered in 2-4 weeks.

Starting price: $8,500 for the first client. You will raise it after that.

Why $8,500: Low enough that a VP can expense it without a procurement cycle. High enough that you are not doing commodity work.

**Tier 2: The Retainer** (monthly, $5,000-$7,500/month)

After the build, you offer ongoing optimization, monitoring, and iteration. The system you built needs tuning. New use cases emerge. They depend on you to keep it running and improving.

This is where the real money lives. Five retainer clients at $6,000/month = $30,000 of recurring baseline revenue before you sell a single new build.

**The conversion play:** Architect every build so it naturally requires ongoing work. Do not build one-and-done scripts. Build systems that compound with attention. Then at delivery, ask: "This is working. Want me to keep optimizing it for $5,000/month?"


## Week 1: Build the Storefront (Days 1-7)

**Budget: $200 max**

The landing page is your entire sales team. One page. No blog. No about section. No portfolio carousel.

**What goes on it:**

Your name. What you do in one sentence. Three types of problems you solve (not features, problems). One testimonial or proof point. A way to book a call.

Use a simple tool. Carrd ($19/year) or a single-page site on your existing domain. Do not overthink this.

**The sentence that matters most:**

"I build AI systems that replace expensive manual processes. Fixed price. Delivered in weeks, not months."

That is it. Ship it by Sunday.

**Also this week:**

Update your LinkedIn headline to match the offer. Something like: "I build AI systems that save companies thousands per month. Fixed price."

No "founder of" or "CEO at" or "passionate about." Nobody cares about titles. They care about outcomes.


## Week 2: Build Your Proof Piece (Days 8-14)

**Budget: $0**

You already built the outreach automation pipeline for BetterStory. You already built financial analysis tools for Benji's. You already built multi-agent systems.

Pick the one that is easiest to explain to a non-technical buyer. Write it up as a short case study. Not a blog post. A case study.

Format:

- The problem (what was broken, what it cost)
- What you built (plain language, no architecture diagrams)
- The result (time saved, money saved, process eliminated)
- How long it took

Keep it under 400 words. Put it on your landing page. Turn it into a LinkedIn post.

You are not selling AI. You are selling the before and after.


## Weeks 3-4: Start Conversations (Days 15-28)

**Budget: $0**

This is where most people stall. They want more case studies. A better website. A content strategy. Forget all of that.

You need five conversations per week with people who have budget and a problem.

**Your outreach list, in order:**

1. Jon Crowley at FUSE Create. You already showed him Claude's product tiers. He knows you. Ask him: "Who at FUSE is spending the most time on repetitive work that should be automated?"

2. Drew Lagzdin at Beanfield. Same deal. You have the relationship. Use it.

3. Your 25 years of agency contacts. Every CD, every VP of Marketing, every ops lead you ever worked with. They all have a budget line item bleeding money on manual work.

4. People who engage with your LinkedIn posts. Every like and comment is a warm lead.

**The DM template:**

"Hey [name]. I have been building AI automation systems for the past year and just finished one that [specific result]. Curious if [their company] is dealing with anything similar. Worth a quick call?"

No pitch deck. No capabilities document. No "I would love to explore synergies." Just a human asking a question.

Send five of these per day, Monday through Friday. That is 25 conversations started per week.


## Weeks 5-6: Close the First Deal (Days 29-42)

**Budget: $0**

By now you should have at least 3-5 discovery calls booked. Here is how to run them:

**Ask three questions:**

1. "What is the most painful manual process in your business right now?"
2. "What does that cost you in time and money every month?"
3. "If I could eliminate that in two weeks for a fixed price, would that be worth exploring?"

If the answer to question 3 is yes, you send a one-page proposal. Not a slide deck. One page.

**The proposal format:**

- The problem (in their words, from the call)
- What you will build
- What they get (the deliverable, the outcome)
- Timeline: 2-4 weeks
- Price: $8,500
- Payment: 50% up front, 50% on delivery

Send it within 24 hours of the call. Speed signals competence.


## Weeks 7-10: Deliver and Document (Days 43-70)

**Budget: up to $500 for any tools or API costs**

Build the thing. Use Claude Code. Ship fast. Communicate progress every 3-4 days with a short Loom or written update.

While you deliver, document everything. Not for a blog. For your next sales conversation.

Every build becomes a case study. Every case study becomes a LinkedIn post. Every LinkedIn post starts a conversation. Every conversation becomes a potential client.

This is the flywheel. Brett ran it with Trello and design files. You run it with Claude Code and AI builds.


## Weeks 11-12: Convert to Retainer and Repeat (Days 71-90)

**Budget: $0**

After delivering the first project:

- Get a testimonial (even two sentences over email is fine)
- Add it to your landing page
- Raise your build price to $12,000-$15,000 for the next client
- Pitch the retainer: "I will keep this running, optimized, and evolving for $5,000/month"
- Keep sending five DMs per day

By Day 90, you should have: one completed build, one retainer client ($5K/month), two to three active build conversations, and a clear path to stacking.

**The road to $50K/month from here:**

Months 4-6: Land 2-3 more builds. Convert each to retainer. You now have 3-4 retainer clients ($15K-$30K/month baseline) plus new build revenue on top.

Months 7-9: Raise build prices to $15K-$20K. You now have 4-5 retainers ($20K-$37K/month) plus 1-2 builds per month. You are at or past $50K.

The retainer base is what makes this a business instead of a hustle. Every month, you wake up with $20K-$30K already spoken for before you sell anything new.


## The 2-Week Sprint: Prove This Works (Start Now)

This is the plan before the plan. Before you build anything for a client, you prove the model works by getting three discovery calls booked in 14 days.

**The bottleneck we identified:** It is not sales skill. It is not time. It is not fear of rejection. It is clarity. You could not describe what you sell in one sentence that would make someone say "I need that." Now you can.

**The wedge offer:** You build AI systems that turn meeting transcripts into structured task lists with owners, deadlines, and priorities. Takes 90 seconds. Every company with more than 5 people in meetings has this problem.

**The upsell story:** The transcript tool is the entry point. It leads into Software of You, a chat-based system that tracks contacts, commitments, calendar, and email with no UI and no SaaS subscription. That is the $5,000/month retainer.

**The narrative in one breath:** "Your meetings generate ideas, decisions, and commitments. Most of them disappear. I build the AI that captures everything and turns it into action. Then I build the layer that keeps it all running."


**Days 1-2: Nail the message.**

Write the landing page sentence using the narrative above. Write three proof paragraphs:

1. Practice Better: You teach and build AI systems for a health tech company. What you built. What it replaced. The result.

2. Benji's: You built financial analysis and ops automation for a plant services business. Before and after. Plain language.

3. Software of You: You built a chat-based CRM that replaced 4 SaaS tools. Runs on Claude Code with a local database. No UI. What it does, why it matters.

Keep each under 100 words. Run them through a sharpening pass before publishing.


**Days 3-4: Ship the landing page.**

One page on betterstory.co. The sentence. The three proof blocks. A Calendly link. Nothing else. Ship it ugly. You will improve it after the first client pays you.


**Days 5-10: Send 25 DMs.**

Five per day. Start with people who already know your name. Use this message:

"Hey [name]. I built something that turns meeting transcripts into structured task lists with owners and deadlines. Takes 90 seconds. Most teams I talk to are losing 5-10 hours a week because action items die in someone's notebook. Curious if that rings true at [company]. Happy to show you how it works."

No pitch. No price. No CTA. Just a smart friend sharing something interesting. The conversation starts naturally from there.


**Days 11-14: Run 3 discovery calls.**

One question drives the whole call: "What happens to action items after your team meetings right now?"

Then listen. Then show them the demo. If they want more, send a one-page proposal within 24 hours.


**The only metric that matters: 3 discovery calls booked by Day 14.**

Not closed deals. Not revenue. Three conversations with people who have a problem and a budget. That proves the clarity issue is solved and the model works. Everything else in this document builds on that proof.


**What makes this work: the boundary.**

You are currently giving Practice Better 40 hours a week on a 20-hour contract. Starting immediately, hold the line at 20. The freed-up 20 hours per week is where this entire sprint lives. You are not adding work. You are reclaiming time you were already giving away for free.




**Do not build a content machine.** One LinkedIn post per week is enough. Spend 80% of your time on conversations and delivery, not content.

**Do not offer strategy or consulting.** Sell builds. Sell outcomes. Strategy is what you give away for free to earn trust. The build is what they pay for.

**Do not lower your price.** If someone cannot afford $8,500, they are not your client. Move on.

**Do not wait until the website is perfect.** Ship it ugly. Refine it after the first client pays you.

**Do not take meetings without a clear next step.** Every call ends with either a proposal or a "not right now." Both are fine.


## Your Weekly Scorecard

Track two numbers only:

1. **Outreach conversations started this week** (target: 25)
2. **Discovery calls booked this week** (target: 3)

Everything else is noise. Revenue follows conversations.

You need one client who says yes. Then you need to deliver something so good they cannot imagine going back to the old way. Then you say five words: "Want me to keep going?"

That retainer question is worth $60,000 a year per client. Ask it every time.

That is how I built DesignJoy to $3M. That is how you build BetterStory to $50K/month.
